## Executive Summary

Spotit Wound Care is a digital wound-care workflow application designed to support structured wound capture, assessment, review planning, caseload visibility and reporting.

The app has been created to help healthcare teams organise wound information more clearly, improve visibility of review priorities, support safer follow-up planning, and reduce fragmented paper-based or informal documentation processes.

Spotit is currently available for private demonstration, buyer review and controlled testing. Dashboard access is restricted and granted by approval only. Real patient data should not be entered unless the purchasing or pilot organisation completes its own GDPR, DPIA, information governance, clinical safety, pilot testing and local approval process.

## The Problem

Wound care often involves repeated reviews, wound photography, measurements, dressing plans, escalation decisions, reporting and communication between clinicians or teams. In many services, these steps may be spread across paper notes, photos, spreadsheets, messages or different clinical systems.

This can create challenges such as:

- Difficulty tracking wounds due for review
- Missed or delayed follow-up
- Limited visibility of deteriorating wounds
- Inconsistent wound documentation
- Time spent preparing reports manually
- Challenges handing over wound progress between clinicians or teams
- Risk of incomplete assessment before a patient is added to caseload

## The Spotit Solution

Spotit provides a structured digital workflow for wound-care review and documentation. It is designed to guide users through a clearer process from wound capture to reporting.

Key workflow areas include:

- Secure sign-in and restricted dashboard access
- Patient and caseload visibility
- Wound photo capture workflow
- Manual and automatic measurement workflow concept
- Wound assessment prompts
- Review status tracking
- Deteriorating, overdue, improving, awaiting review, deferred, inactive and discharged visibility
- Patient consent section
- Audit and governance-aware workflow direction
- PDF/report preparation concept
- MDT and GP-system sharing readiness concept
- Access request process for approved users

## Pilot Proposal

A suitable pilot could involve a controlled, non-live or governance-approved test environment where selected staff evaluate Spotit’s workflow and usability.

Suggested pilot stages:

1. Demo review with service leads, wound-care staff or digital team
2. Agreement on pilot scope and data rules
3. Completion of organisation governance checks
4. Controlled user testing with approved accounts
5. Feedback collection on workflow, usability and reporting
6. Review of safety, privacy and operational suitability
7. Decision on wider adoption, licensing, partnership or purchase

## Governance and Safety

Any organisation wishing to use Spotit with real patient data must complete its own:

- GDPR review
- DPIA
- Information governance review
- Clinical safety review
- Data processing agreement
- Staff training plan
- Local policy approval
- Pilot testing
- Risk assessment
- Technical security review

Spotit can support this process by providing product information, workflow demonstration and technical documentation where required.
