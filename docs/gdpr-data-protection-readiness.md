# Spotit GDPR and Data Protection Readiness

## Current Position

Spotit has important privacy and governance foundations, but it should remain a private demo, buyer review and controlled testing app until a deploying organisation completes formal approval for real patient data.

## Included Foundations

- Restricted login and approved access flow
- Admin access request workflow
- HTTPS live deployment
- PostgreSQL cloud database foundation
- Privacy policy page
- Terms of use page
- Data deletion request page
- Patient consent section
- Audit log foundation
- Role permission foundation
- Clinical workflow prompts
- Report export and MDT sharing workflow concept
- Public warning that real patient data requires organisation approval

## Required Before Real Patient Data

- GDPR review
- DPIA
- Data Processing Agreement
- Information governance approval
- Data retention policy
- Data deletion and subject access process
- Backup and restore test
- Incident response plan
- Role-based access policy
- User onboarding and offboarding policy
- Secure image/file storage review
- Penetration or security testing
- Cloud hosting and supplier risk review
- Staff training
- Local SOPs
- Clinical safety case
- Regulatory or medical-device review where applicable

## Buyer / Organisation Responsibility

Any organisation wishing to use Spotit with real patient data must complete its own privacy, information governance, clinical safety and operational approval process before live clinical use.

## Suggested Buyer Wording

Spotit is available for private demonstration and controlled evaluation. Real patient data must not be entered unless the deploying organisation has completed GDPR, DPIA, information governance, clinical safety, security, regulatory and local approval checks.

## Practical Next Step

Use Spotit for controlled demos and buyer discussions. When an organisation is serious about pilot use, agree a formal pilot scope and complete the required governance documents before entering real patient information.
