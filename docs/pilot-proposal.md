# Spotit Pilot Proposal Draft

## Pilot Aim
Test whether Spotit improves consistency, visibility, and handover quality for wound assessment and dressing-change documentation.

## Pilot Setting
Recommended first pilot:
- One care home, wound clinic, or community nursing team
- 5 to 10 clinicians
- Dummy data first, then consented live data only after governance approval

## Pilot Duration
4 to 8 weeks.

## Pilot Measures
- Time spent documenting wound review
- Completeness of wound assessment entries
- Consistency of wound measurements
- Visibility of deteriorating wounds
- Quality of report/handover notes
- Clinician usability feedback

## Required Before Live Data
- Data protection impact assessment
- Clinical safety review
- Named clinical lead
- User access controls
- Audit logging
- Secure photo storage
- Support contact and incident process

## Pilot Success Criteria
- Clinicians can complete wound assessments without paper duplication.
- Active wounds and due reviews are visible in the caseload.
- Wound photos and measurements are linked to the correct patient/wound.
- Reports are clinically useful and easy to export/send.
- No critical safety or privacy issues are identified.
